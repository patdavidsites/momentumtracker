📅 13F Filing Calendar — When New Holdings Data Drops
Hedge funds with over $100M in US equity must file 13F reports within 45 days of each quarter end. These are the exact dates when Aschenbrenner, Druckenmiller and others must reveal their positions. Mark these in your calendar — check hedgefollow.com the day after each deadline for updates.
How to read 13F filings
What it shows: All long positions in US-listed stocks over $100k. Updated quarterly, 45 days after quarter end.
What it hides: Short positions, options (unless exercisable), foreign stocks, cash, private investments, and any trades made within the quarter.
The lag problem: By the time you see the filing, the data is 45-75 days old. Smart money may have already moved. Use filings for thesis confirmation, not as a trade trigger.
Best sources: hedgefollow.com alerts you the moment a filing drops · sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar for raw filings · whalewisdom.com for comparison tools.

Momentum Indicator Key
Score /10
Composite: RSI + MACD + MA position + ROC + divergence check. 8–10 strong uptrend. 5–7 neutral. Below 5 weakening.
RSI (14-day)
Above 70 = overbought — pullback risk. Below 30 = oversold — bounce potential. The trend of RSI matters more than the number itself.
MACD
12-day minus 26-day EMA. Positive = upward momentum. Watch the histogram — shrinking means momentum is fading even if price still rises.
ROC (20-day)
Rate of price change over 20 days. If 5-day ROC is lower than 20-day ROC, momentum is decelerating — a warning before price turns.
MA Position
Price vs 20, 50, 200-day moving averages. Above all three = strong uptrend. Below 200MA = structural downtrend.
Divergence ⚠
Price hits new high but RSI doesn't confirm it. Momentum is failing before price shows it — one of the most reliable early warnings a professional analyst uses.
Alpha vs Sector
How much your holding beats or lags its sector index. Negative alpha in a rising sector means you're in the wrong stock.
Sector Momentum — Label Guide
Supercycle
A multi-year demand surge that overwhelms supply capacity. Examples: memory 1990s, oil 2000s. Not just a rally — a structural shift in the supply/demand balance that can last 3–7 years. Current: DRAM/HBM memory.
Heating up / Heating fast
Momentum is accelerating — more capital is flowing in, price action is strengthening, and institutional interest is visibly increasing. Early-to-mid stage. The time to research before it becomes consensus.
Strong / Very Strong
Momentum is established and confirmed by multiple signals — price, volume, earnings, and analyst revisions all aligned. Safer entry but less upside than early stage.
Accelerating
The rate of growth is itself increasing — revenue growth is getting faster quarter on quarter. One of the most powerful signals in momentum investing. Earnings revisions going up is the purest form.
Positive / Recovering
Upward trend intact but not at full strength. Either early in a recovery or consolidating after a strong run. Worth holding but not the highest conviction entry point.
Neutral / Stable / Mixed
No clear directional signal. Money is neither aggressively entering nor leaving. Often a sector waiting for a catalyst — can break either way. Watch but don't overweight.
Consolidating / Pressured
Sector has pulled back or is moving sideways after a strong run. Can be healthy (digesting gains) or the beginning of a trend reversal. The difference is whether the fundamentals changed.
Weak / Cautious
Momentum has turned negative. Capital is leaving. Worth monitoring if you hold exposure — may need a catalyst to reverse.
Structural decline / Structural exit
The long-term trend is down — not just a cyclical dip but a permanent shift (e.g. coal, office REITs, legacy retail). Professional investors are structurally underweighting these regardless of short-term bounces.
Score /10
Composite of price momentum (ROC), relative fund flows, earnings revision direction, and analyst sentiment. 8–10 = strong structural buy. 6–7 = positive, monitor. 4–5 = neutral, no edge. Below 4 = avoid or exit.
